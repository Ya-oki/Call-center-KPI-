The model in plain language

The desk earns a shared reward pool equal to a set percentage of the net revenue its clients generated. Each person's slice of that pool is set by a contribution score — what they verifiably did and what came of it. Part of the reward is paid now; part is held until next quarter to prove the clients actually stayed.

Pool

Pool = Net desk revenue × Payout rate

Each agent's score

Score = Conduct × (Outcome-weight × Outcome + Activity-weight × Activity)

  • Outcome — net revenue retained & grown on the clients they serviced, blended with whether those clients survived. This is where withdrawals live: money that leaves and doesn't come back lowers retained revenue and survival — so the score nets out withdrawals automatically.
  • Activity — quality-weighted servicing: meaningful calls, talk-time, messages, responsiveness, and coverage of assigned clients. Rewards effort on clients that haven't paid off yet.
  • Conduct — a 0–1 gate. Complaints, compliance issues, or impeding a client's withdrawal pull it down. No one is ever rewarded for blocking a withdrawal — doing so is penalised.

Your payout

Payout = Pool × (your score ÷ total score) → split into paid now and held.

Withdrawals policy (show this to the team): A withdrawal is never scored as an event. If a client takes profit and keeps trading, your reward is untouched. We will never ask you to stop a withdrawal — and impeding one lowers your conduct score. Withdrawals only matter through whether the relationship stays alive and valuable over the quarter.
Why hold a portion back? Retention value is only real if it lasts. The held part releases next quarter when the clients prove they stayed — and is forfeited if they churn or the revenue reverses. It protects the desk from paying for retention that evaporated.
